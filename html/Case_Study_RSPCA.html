RSPCA

Royal Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals

"Adoptapet"

The RSPCA works to prevent animal suffering by investigating reports of cruelty and neglect, providing care and rehabilitation for animals in need, and enforcing animal welfare laws.

The RSPCA Societies in each state and territory run animal rehoming shelters, investigate and prosecute animal cruelty complaints, educate the community on animal welfare and responsible pet ownership, and advocate for changes in policy and practice that improve the welfare of animals.

  • Provides educational programs that help current and potential pet owners understand what is required when adopting and supporting an animal into their home.

  • In RSCPA's effort to rehabilitate neglected animals they regularly list adoptees that are suitable for rehoming.

  • RSPCA is involved in advocacy and lobbying efforts to improve the welfare of animals

Research

While RSCPA is the most reputible welfare and rehoming organisation, their web presence leaves a lot to be desired. The digital form which RSPCA takes pales in comparison to the in person experiences without mentioning the global ad campaigns. Because RSPCA has made such an impact on the now 'mature' generation/s, they have been able to operate for such a period without maintaining or prioritising their digital presence.

RSCPA's current website design is severly outdated and as a result lacks the standards we now expect from digital products.

Overall the website is not accessible or intuitive for the average user to comprehend and prevents the self-service of potential adoptees. The research shows that users have felt it to be difficult to operate and utilize the RSPCA website for adoption purposes.

Positive

  • Presence of educational material
  • Adoption hosting listed in all states of Australia

Negative

  • Convoluted and poorly optimised adoption process.
  • Outdated/distracting homepage & poor information heirarchy

Problem Statement

Issue 01

Key colour usage is confusing.

  • Eye-catching colours should be singular or of similar shades.
  • Over use of highlight colours

Issue 02

Unoptimised Adoption Process

  • "Animal Id" is required to be noted by the user.
  • Appointment is required to be made by phone call.

Issue 03

Poor location filtering

  • No postcode filtering.
  • User must be familiar with location (town/suburb) names.

Issue 04

Poor information heirarchy

  • Pet bio's are large blocks of text which are unlikely to be read.
  • Adoption hours are poorly formatted.

Issue 05

Homepage is disorganised

  • No clear focus point/area for user.
  • Number of secondary categories found in 'nav menu' are too great.
  • Information density on homepage is too high.

Iterations

Original

The current homepage of the website exhibits an inefficient use of space. It lists knowledge articles and campaigns that are not relevant to the typical user.

1st Iteration

The first iteration prioritised the volunteer and adoption sections of the website which we felt was more aligned with what the typical user is to expect.

2nd Iteration

The second iteration refined some details that were missed in the previous version. The adoption cards were revaluated to provide more information before the user navigates away. RSCPA's branding and logo was also reimplemented to establish the identity.
